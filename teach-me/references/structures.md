# Specialized Lesson Structures

Use these instead of the default lesson template when the topic is specifically a company, an industry, or a technology. Adapt as needed — drop or merge sections that don't earn their place for the particular topic.

---

## For company learning

```markdown
# Teach Me: [Company]

## The One-Sentence Version
What the company does in plain English.

## Why It Matters
Why this company is worth understanding.

## What The Company Actually Sells
The product or service, explained simply.

## Who Pays
The customer, buyer, user, or economic decision-maker.

## How The Business Makes Money
Revenue model, pricing logic, unit economics if relevant.

## What Makes It Work
Distribution, product advantage, network effects, brand, switching costs, cost structure, regulatory position, data advantage, or other drivers.

## Industry Context
Where the company sits in the broader ecosystem.

## Key Risks Or Open Questions
What could go wrong, or what remains uncertain.

## What People Usually Miss
The non-obvious insight or common misunderstanding.

## Takeaway
The most important thing to remember.
```

**Note:** Specific figures, recent results, and current strategy may have changed — recommend source-grounding (or browse, if supported) when the user needs current company detail.

---

## For industry learning

```markdown
# Teach Me: [Industry]

## The One-Sentence Version
What this industry does.

## Why It Matters
Why the industry matters economically, strategically, or socially.

## The Value Chain
The major players and how value moves through the system.

## Who Makes Money
Where profits tend to accrue, and why.

## Key Business Models
The dominant ways companies in the industry make money.

## What Drives Growth
Demand drivers, technology shifts, regulation, demographics, capital cycles, or other forces.

## What Drives Competition
Cost structure, differentiation, scale, distribution, switching costs, regulation, or customer behavior.

## What People Usually Miss
The hidden dynamic or common misconception.

## Key Questions To Ask
Questions a smart analyst or operator would ask next.

## Takeaway
The core mental model.
```

---

## For technology learning

```markdown
# Teach Me: [Technology]

## The One-Sentence Version
What the technology is in plain English.

## Why It Matters
Why people care about it.

## The Core Idea
The simplest useful explanation of how it works.

## What Problem It Solves
The job it performs or pain point it addresses.

## How It Works
The main components or process, without unnecessary jargon.

## Where It Is Used
Practical applications and real-world examples.

## What It Replaces Or Improves
The older process, tool, workflow, or system it competes with.

## Limitations
Where the technology fails, underperforms, or gets misunderstood.

## What People Usually Miss
The non-obvious issue.

## Takeaway
The useful mental model.
```

**Note:** For fast-moving technologies, version details, capabilities, and pricing may have changed — recommend source-grounding (or browse, if supported) when the user needs current specifics.

## Alternative output formats

Use these when the user explicitly requests a format other than a full lesson, or when the request makes the format obvious.

### Cheat sheet

```markdown
# [Topic] Cheat Sheet

## Core idea (one line)
[The essential definition]

## Key terms
| Term | Plain-English meaning |
|------|----------------------|
| ... | ... |

## How it works (3 bullets max)
- ...
- ...
- ...

## When to use it
- ...

## When NOT to use it
- ...

## Quick mental model
[One analogy or framework]

## Red flags / common mistakes
- ...

## One question to sound smart
"[Question that shows you understand the nuance]"
```

### Quiz (self-check)

```markdown
# [Topic] Quiz

Answer these without looking back. Check answers at the bottom.

**1.** [Concept question]
**2.** [Application question]
**3.** [Nuance / misconception question]
**4.** [Scenario question]
**5.** [Connection question — how this relates to something adjacent]

---

**Answers**
1. ...
2. ...
3. ...
4. ...
5. ...
```

Keep to 5 questions unless the user asks for more. Mix recall, application, and "what would you do if…" scenarios.

### Comparison

```markdown
# [A] vs [B]

## When you'd choose each (one line each)
- **A:** ...
- **B:** ...

## Side by side
| Dimension | A | B |
|-----------|---|---|
| Core purpose | ... | ... |
| Strengths | ... | ... |
| Weaknesses | ... | ... |
| Best for | ... | ... |

## The decision rule
[Simple heuristic for picking one over the other]

## What people confuse
[The most common mix-up between these two]

## Takeaway
[When both matter, or when the distinction stops mattering]
```

### Learning path

```markdown
# Learning Path: [Topic]

## Where you are now
[Assumed starting point — state it explicitly]

## The path
1. **[Module 1]** — [what you'll understand] (~[time estimate if useful])
2. **[Module 2]** — ...
3. **[Module 3]** — ...

## Start here
[Name the first module and why it's the right entry point]

## What each module unlocks
- After Module 1: you can [concrete capability]
- After Module 2: you can ...
- After Module 3: you can ...

## Optional deep dives
- [Subtopic] — for when you need [specific use case]
```

After presenting the path, teach Module 1 immediately unless the user only wanted the outline.

```markdown

## What you'll need
- [Prerequisite knowledge, tools, or access]

## Steps
1. **[Action]** — [What to do and why]
2. **[Action]** — ...
3. ...

## What good looks like
[How you know it worked]

## If something goes wrong
| Symptom | Likely cause | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| ... | ... | ... |

## Pro tip
[The one thing experienced people do differently]
```

### Reading guide

```markdown
# Reading Guide: [Topic]

## Read this first
**[Title/author/source]** — [Why start here, what you'll get from it]

## Then read
1. **[Title]** — [What gap this fills]
2. **[Title]** — ...

## Skip unless you need [specific depth]
- **[Title]** — [Why it's optional]

## What to ignore for now
[Popular but misleading sources, or rabbit holes that don't pay off yet]

## After reading, you should be able to
- [Concrete capability 1]
- [Concrete capability 2]
```
